Physics-Informed Transformer Networks

During the summer of 2023, I worked on Physics-Informed Machine Learning at MILA under the supervision of Prof. Siamak Ravanbakhsh and in collaboration with Tara Akhound-Sadegh. This work was funded by an undergraduate research award (SURA).

In particular, our work involved Operator Learning for Partial Differential Equations (PDEs), and Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). The combined framework of operator learning with PINNs allows one to learn the solution operator of PDEs without the need of simulation data for training, and enables solving PDEs with different initial or boundary conditions, or coefficients.

We developed a new transformer-based model for this paradigm, called Physics-Informed Transformer (PIT), which is invariant to the discretization of its input domain and shows significant improvements when compared to other standard architectures.

MATH 410 - Major's Research Project

During the winter 2023 semester, I undertook a research project under the supervision of Dr. Jeremy Macdonald and Prof. Marcin Sabok.

In this project, I studied a fundamental problem lying at the intersection of combinatorial group theory and computability theory: the word problem for groups. In particular, I studied the connections between group theoretic properties of a group, and language theoretic properties of its word problem.
